## üìê The X/Twitter Algorithm in 2025

### **What the Algorithm Prioritizes:**
The 'For You' timeline mixes tweets from accounts you follow with recommendations from accounts you don't, scoring and ranking tweets based on past interactions, what similar users engage with, and trending topics

X penalizes posts with external links by reducing their reach, while giving more visibility to tweets with media like images, videos, and GIFs

### **Ranking Factors (Most to Least Important):**
1. **Engagement rate** (replies > retweets > likes)
2. **Recency** (fresh content favored)
3. **Media attachments** (images, videos, polls)
4. **Dwell time** (how long people read)
5. **Author authority** (follower count, verification)
6. **Content type** (text-only posts now outperform video)

### **What Kills Reach:**
‚ùå External links (save for last tweet in threads)
‚ùå Posting irregularly (gaps of 24+ hours)
‚ùå Low engagement (posts with 0 replies)
‚ùå Copying exact text across tweets
‚ùå Spam-like behavior (same link repeatedly)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### **1. Posting without engaging**
- You can't just broadcast‚Äîyou must participate
- If you post 10x/day but engage 0x/day, you'll plateau at 500 followers

### **2. External links in main tweets**
- X penalizes posts with external links
- Solution: Put links in the LAST tweet of threads or in replies

### **3. Inconsistency**
- Posting 20x one day, then nothing for 3 days kills momentum
- Better: 5 tweets daily, every day

### **4. Being too salesy**
- Twitter punishes self-promotion
- 90% value, 10% promotion (if that)

### **5. Ignoring trends**
- Twitter rewards timely content
- Jump on relevant trends (but stay on-brand)

### **6. Not threading enough**
- Threads get 63% more retweets than single tweets
- Use threads 2-3x per week minimum
